Hills in Tamilnadu

Tamil Nadu features many prominent hills and popular hill stations like Ooty, known as the "Queen of Hills," and Kodaikanal, the "Princess of Hills". Other notable locations include the Nilgiri Mountains, which contain Doddabetta Peak, the highest point in Tamil Nadu, and other well-known spots such as Yercaud, Coonoor, Yelagiri, Kolli Hills, and Meghamalai.

Top Three Hills

  • Nilgiri Hills

    • The Nilgiri Hills, or "Blue Mountains," are a mountain range in the Western Ghats of South India, spanning parts of Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Karnataka, and are home to famous hill stations like Ooty and Coonoor. Known for their lush tea plantations, rich biodiversity, and indigenous tribal communities such as the Toda and Kota, the region also features the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve and the iconic Nilgiri Blue Mountain Railway. The highest point in the Nilgiris is Doddabetta, an important peak within this beautiful and culturally significant landscape

  • palani Hills

    • The Palani Hills are a mountain range in Southern India, forming an eastward extension of the Western Ghats in Tamil Nadu and Kerala. They are known for the popular hill station of Kodaikanal, the city of Palani with its sacred Murugan temple, diverse wildlife, and rich natural beauty, including forests and waterfalls. The hills are a significant destination for tourism, religious pilgrimage, trekking, and exploring nature.

  • Shevaroy Hills

    • "Shevaroys" refers to the anglicized name for the Servarayan hills, a mountain range in Tamil Nadu, southern India, near the city of Salem. It is a part of the Eastern Ghats and is also the location of the hill station of Yercaud. The name originates from the local Tamil deity, Servarayan. Visitors to the Shevaroy Hills can enjoy scenic views, nature photography, and bird watching.
